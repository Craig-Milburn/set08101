Growing Up

I left school in 2010 as I had determined I could no longer happily spend time with the people I was surrouned by. I got a job working in a store. This allowed me the money to have my first expierience building a PC.

This sparked an interest which I continuted as a hobby, little did I know how far my interest would take me.

I applied to be a firefighter, and passed all the training but fell short when my mathematics was too slow. I applied to be a CNC and was turned away because my dyslexia would get in the way of my tasks.

While waiting for the return of my application to the CNC I entered a new job working for Survitec as a Technicians assistant. The job was tough going and increadibly physical, 60 hours a week. When the application failed I felt lost. I left the job I was in and began looking inward and decided that I needed to find something I enjoyed to do for the rest of my life. So at 23 years old searching for college courses on the Edinburgh College website I found a level 6 course on Techincal Support. Having an interest in computing and previous expierience with being a techinicans assistant I applied.

I passed the entry interview and was enrolled in the course.
